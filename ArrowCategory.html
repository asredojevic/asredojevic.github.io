Arrow Category Selection

User Research, Experience, and Interface

PROBLEM

'Where are the filters…'

Arrow.com has millions of parts on their site and each product is classified into a category and sub category. Due the size of our product library, after a search term is entered users MUST select a product category to be presented with relevant filters. Through user testing and google analytics I realized users were missing this critical step in the search journey.

RESEARCH

Filter Best Practice

From interviews, I knew the arrow.com user’s ideal experience would be whatever search term they used would trigger the appropriate filters to appear (magic!) but due to time and other constraints I knew that was not something I would be able to solve quickly or pitch to my team as an ‘MVP’ solution.

Instead I wondered if there was a way to solve this problem through the user flow and/ro interface and started to research how and when competitors and other companies with large libraries of products presented their filters.

TESTING

Competitor Analysis

I conducted an unmoderated remote user test for 15 electrical engineers where they were asked to search for a specific part on our website, a competitor’s website and amazon.

The goal of the test was to determine:

  1. When in the user journey should category be displayed ( on the search results page or prior to the search results page)
  2. Where is the best place to display categories and other filters (left rail or horizontal above)

Findings were that user preferred categories to be displayed on the search results page and it felt too ‘disjointed’ being stopped mid way through the search. They preferred the filters on the top and stated that if they were on the left rail they would be overlooked.

SOLUTION

Interface Design

I hypothesized that by pulling the category filter to the top of the page and giving it a different visual treatment, users would engage with it more thus reducing user frustration and time spent evaluating search results. Users would be able to select a category and drill down into subcategories until they were satisfied with the specific filters and/or search results. We tested this feature by running an AB test against the original design.
